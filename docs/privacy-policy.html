Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 13, 2026

Job Search Lens is a local-only Chrome extension for job searching. This policy explains what the extension accesses, how that information is used, and what is not collected or shared.

What the extension does

The extension highlights saved keywords on any website you browse. On LinkedIn Jobs, it also passively dims job cards that LinkedIn itself labels as Viewed, Saved, or Applied, and shows inline company size and follower counts near job titles.

What data is stored

  • Keywords you save.
  • Color choices for those keywords.
  • Extension settings such as pause state, dim-state toggles, and theme preference.

These values are stored locally in chrome.storage.local on your device and are never transmitted anywhere.

What data is not collected

  • No account information is collected.
  • No personal data is transmitted to the developer or any third party.
  • No job application data is uploaded to any server.
  • No analytics, telemetry, tracking pixels, or third-party advertising is used.
  • No remote code is executed.

How page content is used

The extension reads page content locally inside the browser to find text matches for your saved keywords. This runs on any website where the extension has access. On LinkedIn Jobs it additionally detects LinkedIn’s own Viewed, Saved, and Applied labels already rendered on the page, and reads company size and follower-count text that LinkedIn renders inline.

This processing is on-device only. The extension does not send page content, browsing history, or any metadata to any external endpoint.

Permissions

  • contextMenus — add selected text as a keyword via right-click on any page.
  • storage — persist your keywords and settings locally in the browser.
  • tabs — let the popup communicate with the active tab.
  • http://*/* and https://*/* — run keyword highlighting on any website; LinkedIn-specific dimming and company stats remain scoped to LinkedIn Jobs only.

Data sharing

No stored or processed data is sold, rented, transferred, or disclosed to any third party.

Contact

For support or privacy questions, visit the support page or open an issue on the GitHub issue tracker.
